Artist Statement Mar 2026

Where This Work Lives

The spark begins when an emotion becomes truly mine. It does not come from a concept to illustrate or a message to deliver. It begins when something manifests viscerally, before it can be rationally understood, in the gap between what I feel and what I can name.

The process is exploratory. It starts from a sensation that has to be reconstructed from within, through reflection, empathy, attempt. When it becomes precise enough, the visual search begins. From that point on, every element is built to sustain what the work contains, not to embellish it. Light, matter, rhythm, composition. Aesthetics are not decoration. They are what allow tension to take visible form.

Everything exists within a digital environment, CGI, because it gives the work the level of control it demands. I do not use generative AI, not out of ideology, but because it is not equal to what this work requires. Every frame is a decision, every movement intentionally shaped. That is not a selling point but a condition.

The works are silent, conceived that way. What they carry does not need accompaniment, it needs space. The work does not explain. It activates a trigger, a passage from the stomach to the mind.

I seek works that resist neutral viewing, that generate internal friction, a tension between sensation and understanding.

There has never been a better moment or reason to make art. Human connection is scarce, and even the connections we do have are strained. Endless means of communication, and still misunderstanding is daily, even with those who should know us best. Real connection falters because emotions resist translation. They never pass through directly.

The deepest desire is for the work to become a language between people, a shareable form for inner states that find no words. That someone might say, through a piece: this is what I feel. Not only to recognize themselves, but to declare themselves.

We were not lost. We were just disconnected.

Manifesto Mar 2026

Not Only to Recognize Oneself. Also to Declare Oneself.

We live surrounded by means of communication, and yet deep human connection continues to fail.

Emotions are never easy to transmit. However clearly we express ourselves, they still pass through a mind that decodes them, adjusts them, modulates them according to who is listening. They never arrive directly.

And yet emotions are the compass, the fuel, and the engine of what I do. When something truly resonates, for better or worse, it is the stomach that reacts first, before the mind, before words.

That is why I seek visual forms for emotional territories, not to explain them but to bring them closer to a more immediate passage. A bridge between inner worlds, meant to be felt before they are understood.

I am not interested in the work as a code to be decoded but as a trigger, a possibility of contact, a form capable of carrying something where language alone cannot reach.

But the bridge does not end with the relationship between the work and the viewer.

My deeper hope is that a work may also serve this purpose: that someone may feel seen in an image, in a rhythm, in a tension, and find there a way to say:

this is how I feel
this is what I am going through
I wish I could make you understand this condition

Not as a substitute for language, but as a bridge.

My 1/1 video works are entirely crafted with care in digital form. No generative AI. Every element exists to sustain the emotional tension of the work, never to decorate it.
