Silences

A series of travel notes through the world of silence between people.

Each note experiences a kind of silence and the emotion it carries.

They are meant to be felt, not understood.

Silence #001

The Silence That Becomes

There's a silence I don't remember choosing. At first it was yours: your withdrawal, a word I was waiting for that never came, and me there, counting the days. I endured it, and it hurt in a sharp, recognizable way.

Then something shifts, and I can't say when. I stop waiting. Not by decision: there was no moment when I said enough. It's just that one day I realize the silence I was enduring has become mine too. I exercise it without having decided to.

This is where the two blur. From the outside, and often from the inside too, I can no longer tell whether I'm imposing that silence or still enduring it. Perhaps this is always what happens in a couple: each person believes they're suffering the other's silence while cultivating their own, and neither of them knows any longer where their part ends.

I don't say this as blame, nor as a lament. I say it because it's exact: certain silences are neither imposed nor endured. They become. And when I realize it, I am already made of the same matter.

Silence #002

The Silence I Keep

There's something I know and don't tell you. Not a great secret, not a lie: just a word I could lay between us every evening, and every evening I put it off. I keep it aside, I look at it now and then, then I set it back where it was. You don't know it exists, and I live beside you with that word in my pocket.

At first I convinced myself it was for you. To spare you, to not ruin an evening that was going well. Staying silent seemed the kind thing, almost a courtesy, and for a while I believed it.

Then I realize it's no longer only for you. Inside that silence I'm safe too, far from a conversation I don't want to open. The longer I put it off, the more the word weighs; and the more it weighs, the less I can say it. It's no longer something I'm keeping quiet: it's a room I've shut myself into, and I've left you outside as well.

I no longer know whether what I keep is your peace or mine. I only know that every day I don't speak, the word becomes a little more impossible, and the unsaid a little more true. Some things aren't kept silent forever: they're kept silent until they can no longer be said.

Silence #003

The Silence We Know

There's a crowded table, and among the voices the glance I send you. Someone starts to speak, and before the sentence even ends our eyes have already met. No one around notices. In the general murmur a whole conversation flows between us, without a sound, and we both hear it from beginning to end.

We never agreed on this language. It grew on its own, one year over the next, until a movement of the eyes was enough to say what once took a whole evening. There's something only we find funny, and I couldn't tell it to anyone without snuffing it out. There's no need to tell it. I keep it there, between you and me.

Then I see the other side of this being quiet together. The same glance that holds us draws a line: on one side there are two of us, on the other the whole room. Our understanding is warmth for us and a closed door for those around us. I no longer know whether I chose this closeness or it grew on me.

The things we say best don't pass through words. They pass through that nothing that sparks over everyone's heads and reaches only you.

The room is full, and inside there's no one but the two of us.
