The Stage

Acts of a Lucid Silence

The Stage — Acts of a Lucid Silence is a trilogy of silent video works exploring the verbally unspoken through essential, suspended scenes conceived as an inner theatre. Each act holds a tension seeking form without fully resolving. The center is not the action itself, but its edge: what presses to emerge, what comes at a cost, what remains restrained. The scene is reduced to its essentials: fixed camera, dark space, controlled light, very few elements. Silence is not absence, but pressure. Created entirely in digital form and without the use of generative AI, the trilogy treats video as an autonomous and self-contained medium.

ACT I

I Have To

A concrete wall, isolated in darkness. An invisible gesture carves a word into the surface, letter by letter. Only friction.
